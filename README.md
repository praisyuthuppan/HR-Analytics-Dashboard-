# HR-Analytics-Dashboard-
HR Analytics Dashboard | Employee Attrition Analysis | Tableau
Project Overview

This project focuses on analyzing employee attrition and workforce behavior using Tableau. The objective is to transform raw HR data into actionable insights that help organizations understand the factors influencing employee turnover and support data-driven decision-making. The dashboard provides an interactive view of workforce demographics, employee distribution, job satisfaction, and attrition trends across different departments, job roles, age groups, and educational backgrounds.

Dataset Information

The analysis was conducted using an HR dataset containing information on 1,470 employees. The dataset includes employee demographics, department details, job roles, education fields, job satisfaction levels, and attrition status. These attributes were utilized to identify patterns and trends related to employee retention and workforce management.

Dashboard Features
The dashboard was designed to provide a comprehensive overview of workforce performance and employee attrition through interactive visualizations and key performance indicators (KPIs). Key metrics include:
- Total Employees
- Active Employees
- Employees Who Left
- Attrition Rate
- Employee Distribution
- Job Satisfaction Analysis
- Department-wise Attrition
- Age Group Analysis
- Gender-wise Attrition
- Education Field Analysis
- 
🔍 Key Insights
- The organization has an overall attrition rate of 16.12%, indicating a moderate level of employee turnover.
- The Research & Development (R&D) department experiences the highest attrition, highlighting potential challenges in retaining technical talent.
- Employees aged 25–34 years show the highest turnover rate, reflecting greater career mobility among early-career professionals.
- Male employees contribute to a larger share of attrition, primarily due to their higher representation in the workforce.
- Sales Executives, Research Scientists, and Laboratory Technicians constitute a major portion of the workforce and contribute significantly to overall attrition.
- Employees with educational backgrounds in Life Sciences and Medical fields account for a considerable share of employee exits, likely due to attractive external career opportunities.
 - Job satisfaction levels vary across different job roles, suggesting a potential relationship between employee satisfaction and retention.
- Attrition patterns differ across departments, age groups, job roles, and education fields, indicating multiple factors influencing workforce turnover.
  
💡 Business Impact
- Enables HR teams to identify employee groups with a higher risk of attrition and take proactive retention measures.
- Supports the development of targeted retention strategies for departments with higher turnover, particularly the R&D department.
- Helps organizations design career development and employee engagement programs for employees in the 25–34 age group.
- Provides valuable insights into workforce behavior, supporting data-driven HR and management decisions.
- Assists in understanding the impact of job satisfaction on employee retention and workplace engagement.
- Improves workforce planning by identifying departments and job roles that require focused retention efforts.
- Supports strategic talent management by highlighting key factors contributing to employee turnover.
- Helps reduce recruitment, onboarding, and training costs by addressing attrition drivers proactively.
- Enhances organizational stability and productivity through improved employee retention initiatives.
- Serves as a decision-support tool for building long-term workforce and talent management strategies.
